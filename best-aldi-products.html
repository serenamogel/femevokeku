18 Best Aldi Products That Weve Tasted

Wondering what to buy at Aldi? We regularly wander the aisles, looking for the best Aldi products to try for our taste tests or sample just for fun. (We live in a tough world, sometimes you have to find joy in the simple things, like a new frozen shrimp.) So, you can trust us when it comes to the best Aldi buys. 

Here are the 18 best Aldi products, according to our taste tests. 

Best Aldi Granola

Simply Nature Dark Chocolate & Sea Salt GranolaAldi

Much like Trader Joe’s, Aldi is a great store if you’re looking for unique takes on snacks. And this is one of the best Aldi products that really made us raise an eyebrow. This dark chocolate granola is bitter like cocoa, actually salty, and unusual in a way that keeps you grabbing for cluster after cluster. And speaking of clusters, these are solid granola clusters that hold together—no dust in this bag.

Best Aldi Bratwurst

Deutsche Kuche Uncured Bavarian BratwurstAldi

Aldi’s Bavarian brats beat out Trader Joe’s Bavarian brats in a head-to-head competition within our best brat taste test. They may not be as big, but their flavor is much better. As managing editor Gwynedd Stuart described it in her review, “You get pepper and herbs and a little bit of underlying sweetness.” They’re classic Bavarian brats. Enjoy one with a boot full of Oktoberfest.

Best Aldi GF Tortillas

Live G Free SpinachInstacart

We included two gluten-free tortillas from Aldi in our best gluten-free tortillas ranking—the Live G Free Original flavor and the Live G Free Spinach flavor. The spinach has the edge, though, thanks to the sheer amount of spinach, dill, and garlic flavor the gluten-free tortilla packs. However, if you’re in the mood for something plain, the original flavor won’t let you down. They’re big, they’re stretchy, and they’ll hold all your fillings together.

Best Aldi Beef Jerky

Simms Original BiltongAldi

While Aldi’s Simms brand of regular beef jerky is totally acceptable, the biltong (a South African style of dried meat) is much more interesting. It tastes like Italian bresaola. It’s very meat-forward, extremely thinly sliced, and easy to eat handful after handful. It’s one of the best Aldi products for on-the-job snacking, perfect for slipping into a desk drawer.
