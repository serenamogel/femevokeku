    Deborah Snyder Producer

    Before starting her career as a film producer, Deborah worked at the New York advertising agency Backer Spielvogel Bates. In 1996, she hired Zack Snyder to direct a commercial for Reebok, hoping to create a commercial with a cinematic feel. Later in 2004, the couple became the co-founders of Cruel and Unusual Films alongside their producing partner Wesley Coller.

    Around 2007, Deborah held the position of executive producer for Zack Snyder’s 2007 film 300, an adaptation of Frank Miller’s same-titled graphic novel. She was also the producer of the 2009 film adaptation of the graphic novel Watchmen.

    She has worked as an executive producer for the 2010 computer-animated film Legend of the Guardians: The Owls of Ga’Hoole, which is based on Guardians of Ga’Hoole, a series of children’s fantasy books by Kathryn Lasky.  Deborah produced Sucker Punch, which was co-written, co-produced, and directed by her husband. The film was the first to credit their company Cruel and Unusual Films as a co-producer.

    On January 29, 2019, Zack Snyder declared that he has signed on to helm Army of the Dead, a zombie horror-thriller, for Netflix. Deborah is set to co-produce with her husband and partner, Wesley Coller, through their newly rebranded production company, Stone Quarry.
