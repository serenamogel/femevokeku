Urban Meyer Wiki, Salary, Daughter, Wife, Net Worth &amp; Coach Record

Urban Meyer is a top-notch American football coach associated with the Ohio State Buckeyes football team. His excellent contributions to American college football cannot be written off easily. He twice won the BCS National Championship with the University of Florida football team; as well as a national championship winner with Buckeyes Ohio State University. Besides coaching football, Meyer also played football as a fullback for the University of Cincinnati football team. Learn more about his biography, coaching background, family, net worth and other facts below.

Biography of Urban Meyer

Urban Meyer was born in Toledo, Ohio, United States on July 10, 1964. He was raised along with his other siblings by their parents, Gisela Meyer and her husband Bud Meyer. He attended a private high school called Saint John High School located in Ashtabula, Ohio. Meyer was a two-sport athlete; he played baseball and football for his school team.

After graduating, he enrolled at the University of Cincinnati where he played college football as a fullback and baseball. Meyer earned a bachelor’s degree in psychology in 1986. He later earned a master’s degree in sports administration from The Ohio State University.

Coaching career and record

He entered coaching in 1985 when he interned as a defensive back coach at Saint Xavier High School in Cincinnati, Ohio. After that, he landed his first college coaching job as a graduate assistant, tight ends coach at Ohio State. He worked with trainer Earle Bruce. Later, he spent thirteen years as an assistant coach for the Illinois State football team, five years at Notre Dame, and three years at Colorado State.

Urban Meyer made his breakthrough in 2001 when he became the head coach of the Bowling Green Falcons football team. After spending two seasons with Bowling Green, Meyer took on another coaching role with Utah Utes football in 2003. He led the team to victory at the Skyline Conference Championship as well as the Fiesta Bowl in 2005. After his great accomplishments in Utah, the University of Notre Dame and the University of Florida vied for his services. Meyer then accepted Florida’s offer, becoming head coach of the Florida Gators in 2005. He signed with the Gators on a $14 million deal.

While in Florida, Meyer led the Gators to win 5 championships, including a Sugar Bowl, two Outback Bowls, and two BCG National Championships. After the 2010 season, Meyer resigned as head coach of the Gators. After that, he briefly worked as a football commentator and analyst for ESPN. In November 2011, Urban Meyers signed a contract with Ohio State Buckeyes. He signed a six-year contract that includes $4 million in annual salaries and $2.4 million in retention.

Meyer led Ohio State with a 12-0 record in his freshman year in 2012. The following year, he won the Orange Bowl with the team and the Sugar Bowl in 2014. In 2015, he won the Fiesta Bowl and gets a contract extension until 2020. In the 2016 season, he won the Eastern Conference. Champion as he also won the Cotton Bowl title in 2017.

In August 2018, Urban Meyer was placed on administrative leave following reports that he had knowledge of domestic abuse involving his former assistant coach, Zach Smith, and his wife. An investigative panel reviewed the evidence, and the Ohio board of directors ruled that Meyer should miss the first three games of the 2018 season.

What is Urban Meyer’s net worth and salary?

When it comes to football, Urban Meyer has been through thick and thin. He has been active for over two decades and has won several awards for his outstanding performances. He signed a deal worth $24 million with the Florida Gators in 2009 which contributed to his massive net worth. Meyer is currently one of the highest paid college football coaches in America. He earns an annual salary of $5.8 million and a net worth of $20 million.
