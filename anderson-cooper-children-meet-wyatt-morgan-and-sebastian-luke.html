Biography of Anderson Cooper

Anderson Hays Cooper is an American broadcast journalist and political commentator, born on June 3, 1967, in New York City. He is the son of fashion designer and socialite Gloria Vanderbilt and writer Wyatt Emory Cooper. Cooper grew up in a wealthy family, but his life was not without tragedy. His father passed away when he was just ten years old, and his brother Carter committed suicide in 1988.

Cooper attended Yale University, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science. After completing his education, he started his career as a fact-checker for Channel One News, a news program aimed at young adults. He eventually became a correspondent for the show, covering events such as the genocide in Rwanda and the war in Bosnia.

In 1995, Cooper joined ABC News, where he worked as a co-anchor for World News Now and a correspondent for 20/20. Cooper gained recognition for his coverage of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, during which he reported live from New Orleans. The following year, he left ABC News to join CNN, where he currently anchors the news broadcast show Anderson Cooper 360°.

In addition to his work at CNN, Cooper serves as a correspondent for 60 Minutes on CBS News. He has won numerous awards for his reporting, including several Emmy Awards and a Peabody Award. Cooper is also a bestselling author, having written several books, including “Dispatches from the Edge” and “The Rainbow Comes and Goes,” which he co-wrote with his mother.

Cooper is openly gay and has been an advocate for LGBTQ rights. He is also involved in various charitable organizations, including the Elton John AIDS Foundation and UNICEF. Despite facing personal tragedies in his life, Anderson Cooper has built a successful career as a respected journalist and continues to make a positive impact on the world through his work and advocacy.

Does Anderson Cooper have any children? Yes, Anderson has two children; Wyatt Morgan Cooper, Sebastian Luke Maisani-Cooper.
