Daler Singh is a veteran Indian Punjabi singer. He is famous for his melodious voice and the way he sings the bhangra and pop songs. He is a songwriter, author, and record producer. He is also a businessman. He is better known for his Indian pop music and bhangra songs. He is known for his dance songs as well.

The all-time favorite singer was born on 18th August 1967 in Patna, Bihar, India. He formed his own group along with his brothers, cousins, and friends in 1991. In 1994, he won the Voice of Asia International Ethnic and Pop Music Contest in Almaty, Kazakhstan.

Career

The singer, Daler Mehndi is a man who worked hard to reach the peak of success. He is known for his incredibly amazing singing talent. He has sung a variety of bhangra songs and pop as well for which he got immense popularity in his career life. Here we can say that the multitalented star is a globally recognized man. There is no doubt that he has a huge network and fan base across the world.

The singer is known for his notable songs, such as; ‘Tunak Tunak Tun’, ‘Bolo Ta Ra Ra’, ‘Hojaegi Bhalle Bhalle’, and many others that are worth listening to. Moreover, the singer sang songs for Indian movies, such as; ‘Na Na Na Re’ film ‘Mirtyudhand’ starring Amitabh Bachchan. There are many films for which he has sung songs.

Apart from his musical career, he is known as a great author. Moreover, Daler Mehndi is also a successful businessman as he launched his maiden Record label ‘DRecords’ in the year 2020. The record label comprises several artists including Hussain Baksh and Safari Boys.

The singer established his identity in the music industry and became famous for his contribution to the music industry at a national and international level. He is also a philanthropist and has been supporting many charitable organizations.

The financially independent man has gained lasting success and fame in his career life. And the best is, Daler Mehndi has won many awards and honors for his performances. He won the Voice of Asia International Ethnic and Pop Music Contest in Almaty, Kazakhstan in 1994.
